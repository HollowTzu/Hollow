⚡ STRIKING INSTITUTIONAL NOTE: THE "TARGET REVISION" DECEPTION
Do not mistake near-term bank target cuts (e.g., sell-side desks lowering short-term targets to ~$4,500/oz) as a structural trend reversal. Target revisions this cycle are purely driven by tactical real yield mechanics as the Fed maintains a higher-for-longer stance. Beneath this noise, central banks continue un-hedged structural accumulation (~850+ tonnes projected for 2026), and sovereign fiscal deficit risks remain unmitigated. The institutional desk consensus remains clear: near-term real yield suppression creates long-term structural value.
