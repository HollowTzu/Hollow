FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS — GOLD & USD · Q3 2026 (JUL–SEP)
Arrows show the current directional pull on USD value from each factor — not a gold call by itself. The debt figure below is fetched live from Treasury on every page load. MONETARY & FISCAL POLICY
▲ USD — Fed funds held at 3.50–3.75% under a "higher-for-longer" stance; some desks (BofA, Deutsche Bank) now pencil in further hikes rather than cuts as 2026 inflation runs near 3.6%. Tighter policy and elevated real yields are a direct headwind for gold and support near-term dollar strength.

▼ USD — Underneath that, the fiscal picture is structurally worse: FY2026 deficit tracking ~$1.9T (~5.8% of GDP), debt-to-GDP over 100% and climbing toward CBO's projected 120% by 2036. That's the slow-moving, structural case for currency debasement that keeps central banks buying gold regardless of where rates sit this quarter.

GEOPOLITICAL RISK & SENTIMENT
▲ USD — Despite active US-Iran tensions and oil price pressure, capital has recently favored the dollar and Treasuries over gold as the primary safe-haven — the hawkish Fed narrative is currently dominating the geopolitical bid, which is the less typical pairing. Gold slipped to ~$4,010 on July 17 even as tensions stayed elevated.

Watch: this can flip fast — a genuine Strait-of-Hormuz disruption or a NATO-adjacent escalation would likely reassert gold's safe-haven bid over the dollar.
SUPPLY & DEMAND (GOLD)
▼ USD — Central banks bought 244t net in Q1 2026 (+3% y/y), with the World Gold Council forecasting ~850t for the full year; a record 45% of surveyed central banks plan to add more gold over the next 12 months. This is structural reserve diversification away from the dollar, not a trade — it doesn't reverse on a bad week. Mine supply growth remains modest (~2% y/y), so it's not being met by a supply response.
